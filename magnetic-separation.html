Magnetic Separation

Magnetic separation takes advantage of a substance's magnetic properties. Strong magnets are used on mixtures to separate the substance with magnetic properties from the substance without.

This diagram shows how magnetic separation is used to separate mixtures. The strong magnet attracts the magnetic substance and removes it from the non-magnetic one. In this case, the magnet is used to separate iron filings from sand. The magnet attracts the iron filings and leaves the sand behind, effectively separating the two.
