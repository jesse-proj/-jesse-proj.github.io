Sublimation

Sublimation is a process in chemistry which a solid transforms into a gas without melting first in order to form a liquid. This happens when the solid's triple point, or the pressure and temperature whereby all three phases—solid, liquid, and gas—coexist in equilibrium, are less than those.

This diagram shows the process of sublimation. The ammonium chloride turns into gas, which is then collected in the cotton plug. This can be used to separate mixtures. In the case where chalk powder is mixed in with the ammonium chloride, the chalk powder will remain solid whilst the ammonium chloride turns into gas.
