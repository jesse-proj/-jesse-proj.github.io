Threshing

Separating the grain out from chaff is done through threshing. A mixture of wheat and husk is dumped from a height during this operation. The husk is then carried along by the breeze and collects in a mound a short distance away. Due to its reduced weight, the husk is transported by the air flow and forms a distinct heap. On the threshing floor, where the useable portion is being separated out, the process of threshing is carried out mechanically by beating the grain with a flail.
