Sieving

Sieving takes advantage of a mixture's components being different sizes. A sieve is used to separate the different substances, with the smaller particles falling through the holes of the sieve and leaving the larger substance behind.

This diagram shows how sieving can separate a mixture of rocks and sand. The rocks and sand are put onto a sieve and the sand falls through the holes, leaving the rocks behind. This separates the mixture into two different substances.
