Decantation

Decantation takes advantage of the difference between the densities of the components present in a mixture. The less dense substance forms the top layer and the denser substance stays under it. The top layer is then poured into a different container.

This diagram shows how Decantation is used to separate water and a denser substance such as dirt. The water forms the top layer and the denser substance stays below, allowing for the transfer and separation of the top layer to a different container.

Decantation can also be used to separate two substances that do not mix well such as oil and water.
